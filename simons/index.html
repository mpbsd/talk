Main results

Lemma 1
If $M$ is compact and has non-negative sectional curvature (for all $2$-planes), then at every point of $M$ we have \[ \nabla{A}=0 \quad\text{and}\quad (\lambda_{i}-\lambda_{j})^{2}K_{ij}=0 \quad\text{for all}\quad{i,j}. \] In particular, the eigenvalues of $A$ are constant (where the field of unit normals $\xi$ is defined).

Lemma 2
If $M$ has non-negative sectional curvature, and \[ f=\text{trace}\left(A^{2}\right) \] is constant on $M$, then we have the same conclusions as Lemma 1.
Lemma 3
Under the assumptions of Lemma 1 or Lemma 2, either $M$ is totally umbilical or $A$ has exactly two distinct constants as eigenvalues at every point.
Theorem 1
Let $M$ be a complete Riemannian manifold of dimension $n$ with non-negative sectional curvature, and $\phi:M\to{\mathbb{R}^{n+1}}$ an isometric immersion with constant mean curvature into an Euclidean space $\mathbb{R}^{n+1}$. If $f=\text{trace}\left(A^{2}\right)$ is constant on $M$, then $\phi(M)$ is of the form $\mathbb{S}^{p}\times\mathbb{R}^{n-p}$, $0\leqslant{p}\leqslant{n}$, where $\mathbb{R}^{n-p}$ is an $(n-p)$-dimensional subspace of $\mathbb{R}^{n+p}$, and $\mathbb{S}^{n}$ is a sphere in the Euclidean subspace perpendicular to $\mathbb{R}^{n-p}$. Except for the case $p=1$, $\phi$ is an imbedding.
Corollary 1
If $M$ is, in particular, minimal in Theorem 1, then $\phi(M)$ is a hyperplane and $\phi$ is an imbedding.
Corollary 2
Let $M$ be a connected compact Riemannian manifold of dimension $n$ with non-negative sectional curvature. If $\phi:M\to{\mathbb{R}^{n+1}}$ is an isometric immersion with constant mean curvature, then $\phi(M)$ is a hypersphere and $\phi$ is an imbedding.
Theorem 2
Let $M$ be an $n$-dimensional complete Riemannian manifold with non-negative sectional curvature, and $\phi:M\to{\mathbb{S}^{n+1}}$ an isometric immersion with constant mean curvature. If $f=\text{trace}\left(A^{2}\right)$ is constant on $M$, then either:
  • $\phi(M)$ is a great or small sphere in $\mathbb{S}^{n+1}$, and $\phi$ is an imbedding; or
  • $\phi(M)$ is a product of spheres $\mathbb{S}^{p}(r)\times\mathbb{S}^{q}(s)$, and for $p\notin\{1,n-1\}$, $\phi$ is an imbedding.
Corollary 1
If $M$ is, in particular, minimal in Theorem 2, then $\phi(M)$ is a great sphere or \[ \mathbb{S}^{p}\left(\sqrt{p/n}\right)\times\mathbb{S}^{n-p}\left(\sqrt{\left(n-p\right)/n}\right). \]
Corollary 2
Let $M$ be a connected compact Riemannian manifold of dimension $n$ with non-negative sectional curvature. If $\phi:M\to{\mathbb{S}^{n+1}}$ is an isometric immersion with constant mean curvature, then the conclusion of Theorem 2 holds.
Corollary 3
Let $M$ be a connected compact minimal hypersurface immersed in $\mathbb{S}^{n+1}$. If $M$ has positive sectional curvature, then $M$ is imbedded as a great sphere.
